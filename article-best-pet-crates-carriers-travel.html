2. What to Look For

  • Airline approval – check size restrictions (most airlines allow carry‑on if carrier fits under seat).
  • Ventilation – mesh windows for airflow and visibility.
  • Security – zippers that lock or sturdy latches.
  • Safety – for car travel, choose a crash‑tested crate or carrier.
  • Size – your pet should be able to stand, turn around, and lie down comfortably.
  • Weight – lightweight carriers are easier to carry.

4. How to Size a Crate or Carrier

  • Length: Measure from nose to base of tail. Add 2‑3 inches.
  • Height: Measure from floor to top of head when standing.
  • Width: Measure widest part of shoulders or hips.
  • Your pet should be able to stand, turn around, and lie down comfortably.

5. How to Acclimate Your Pet to a Crate/Carrier

  1. Leave the carrier open at home with treats and a familiar blanket inside.
  2. Encourage your pet to explore it voluntarily.
  3. Start with short periods with the door closed, while you're present.
  4. Gradually increase duration and take short car trips.
  5. Use pheromone sprays (Adaptil, Feliway) to reduce anxiety.

7. What to Avoid

  • Carriers that are too small – uncomfortable and dangerous.
  • Cheap zippers – can break, allowing escape.
  • Carriers without ventilation – can lead to overheating.
  • Non‑crash‑tested crates for car travel – can become projectiles in an accident.
