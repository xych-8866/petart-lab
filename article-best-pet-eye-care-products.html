4. How to Clean Your Pet's Eyes

  1. Wash your hands before touching your pet's eyes.
  2. Use a clean eye wipe or cotton ball moistened with a pet-safe eye wash.
  3. Gently wipe from the inner corner to the outer corner (in the direction of hair growth).
  4. Use a fresh wipe for each eye to prevent cross-contamination.
  5. If using drops, follow the product instructions and your vet's guidance.

5. How to Prevent Tear Stains

  • Clean your pet's eyes daily
  • Use tear stain remover products regularly
  • Maintain a healthy diet – some tear stains are linked to diet
  • Keep hair around the eyes trimmed (for long-haired breeds)
  • Monitor for underlying conditions (allergies, infections)

7. What to Avoid

  • Human eye drops – can be harmful to pets
  • Harsh chemicals – can irritate the eye
  • Cotton swabs – can damage the cornea
  • Using the same wipe for both eyes – can spread infection

8. Your Action Plan

  2. Establish a daily eye cleaning routine
  3. Monitor for signs of discharge, redness, or discomfort
  4. Consult your vet if you notice any persistent issues
