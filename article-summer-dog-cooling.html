How to Keep Your Dog Cool in Summer: Heat Safety Tips

By PetArt Lab · May 29, 2026

Key Takeaways

  • Never leave your dog in a parked car—even for a minute
  • Provide constant access to fresh, cool water
  • Use cooling mats and vests for outdoor walks
  • Know the signs of heatstroke: excessive panting, drooling, lethargy

Summer is a wonderful time for outdoor adventures with your dog, but rising temperatures can pose serious risks. Dogs can overheat quickly, and heatstroke can be fatal if not caught early. Here's how to keep your pup safe and cool all summer long.

1. Water, Water, and More Water

Hydration is your dog's first line of defense against the heat. Always carry a portable water bottle and bowl when you're out. At home, consider a pet water fountain—many dogs drink more from running water.

3. Recognize the Warning Signs

Excessive panting, thick drool, bright red gums, vomiting, or sudden lethargy are all signs of heatstroke. Move your dog to shade immediately, offer small amounts of cool (not ice-cold) water, and contact your vet.

4. Adjust Your Walk Schedule

Walk early in the morning or late in the evening when pavement is cooler. Place the back of your hand on the ground—if it's too hot for you, it's too hot for your dog's paws.
