3. How to Remove Hair from Car Interiors (Tricky Area)

  • Use a rubber broom (dry) to sweep hair into piles.
  • Follow with a vacuum that has a crevice tool.
  • For stubborn hair, wear a rubber glove and run your hand over the fabric – static lifts the hair.
  • Use a pumice stone (for upholstery) – but test on a hidden spot first.

4. Reducing Shedding at the Source (Less Cleaning Later)

  • Brush daily during shedding seasons using an undercoat rake or deshedding tool (Furminator).
  • Feed omega‑3 fatty acids – improves skin and coat health, reduces excessive shedding.
  • Bathe with a deshedding shampoo – loosens dead undercoat in the bath, so it goes down the drain, not on your sofa.

6. What NOT to Buy

  • Cheap tape rollers with small sheets – you'll go through a roll in one couch.
  • Vacuums that claim "pet" but have no brushroll shutoff – hair wraps around the brush and burns the belt.
  • Rubber brooms that aren't self‑cleaning – you'll spend more time removing hair from the broom than from the floor.
