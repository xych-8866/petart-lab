Common Small Ailments in Dogs and How to Prevent Them Naturally

By PetArt Lab · May 23, 2026

Key Takeaways

  • Spot early signs of ear infections, hot spots, and digestive issues
  • Use natural, vet-approved home care techniques
  • Keep a pet first-aid kit at home
  • Know when to seek professional veterinary help

Dogs can't tell us when something feels off, but their bodies often give us clues. Learning to spot early signs of common ailments can save your dog discomfort—and save you expensive vet bills.

1. Ear Infections

Signs include head shaking, scratching at ears, redness, or a bad smell. Prevent them by keeping ears dry after baths and swimming. Clean ears gently with a vet-approved solution—never use cotton swabs deep in the ear canal.

2. Hot Spots

These red, inflamed patches of skin can appear suddenly and spread fast. They're often triggered by allergies, moisture trapped in the coat, or excessive licking. Keep the area clean and dry, and prevent your dog from licking it.

3. Digestive Upsets

Occasional vomiting or diarrhea can happen from eating something they shouldn't. For mild cases, a bland diet of boiled chicken and rice for 24 hours can help. Probiotic supplements may also support gut health. If symptoms persist beyond 24 hours, see your vet.

4. Dental Problems

Bad breath isn't just unpleasant—it can signal dental disease. Regular brushing with dog-specific toothpaste and dental chews can help keep teeth clean. Annual dental check-ups are recommended for senior dogs.
