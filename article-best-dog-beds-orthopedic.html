1. What Makes a Bed "Orthopedic"?

  • High‑density memory foam – conforms to the dog's body, relieving pressure points on hips and elbows.
  • Supportive egg‑crate or flat surface – prevents joint compression.
  • Waterproof or removable washable cover – incontinence or accidents happen with seniors.
  • Non‑slip bottom – keeps the bed in place on hard floors.

3. How to Choose the Right Size & Thickness

  • Thickness: For dogs <40 lbs, 3‑4 inches is sufficient. For 40‑80 lbs, 5‑6 inches. For >80 lbs, 7+ inches (Big Barker).
  • Size: Measure your dog from nose to tail and add 6‑12 inches. The bed should be large enough that they can fully stretch out.
  • Cover: Choose a bed with a removable, machine‑washable cover. Senior dogs have accidents, and you'll thank yourself later.

5. What to Avoid

  • Beds that are too thin (<2 inches): They offer no joint support and will compress quickly.
  • Polyester filling (not foam): Does not provide consistent support; flattens in weeks.
  • Non‑removable covers: You cannot clean them, leading to bacteria and odors.
