Best Cat Scratching Posts to Save Your Furniture

By PetArt Lab · May 29, 2026

Key Takeaways

  • Scratching is a natural, healthy behavior—never punish your cat for it
  • Choose tall, sturdy posts that allow full-body stretching
  • Sisal rope posts last longer and satisfy scratching instincts better than carpet
  • Place posts near sleeping areas and in high-traffic rooms

Every cat owner knows the frustration of finding new claw marks on the sofa. But scratching isn't bad behavior—it's essential for your cat's physical and mental health. The key is providing the right scratching posts in the right places.

1. Why Cats Scratch

Scratching helps cats shed old claw sheaths, stretch their muscles, and mark territory through scent glands in their paws. A good scratching post satisfies all these needs, keeping your furniture safe.

2. What Makes a Great Scratching Post

The best posts are tall enough for your cat to fully stretch (at least 30 inches), sturdy enough that they won't tip over, and wrapped in sisal rope—the material cats prefer most. Avoid carpet-covered posts; they can confuse cats into thinking your carpet is also fair game.

3. Placement Is Everything

Put scratching posts where your cat already spends time—near their bed, by windows, and in the living room. If they're scratching the arm of your sofa, place a post directly in front of that spot, then gradually move it away once they've switched.

4. Encourage, Don't Discourage

Use catnip or treats to attract your cat to the new post. Praise them when they use it. Never force their paws onto it or punish them for scratching elsewhere—positive reinforcement works best.
