1. Why Use Training Pads?

  • Consistency – provides a consistent elimination spot, which is key for house‑training.
  • Damage control – protects floors and carpets from accidents.
  • Senior pets – useful for dogs with mobility issues or incontinence.
  • Apartment dwellers – a convenient alternative for late‑night potty breaks.
  • Weather protection – when it's too hot, cold, or rainy to go outside.

4. How to Train Your Puppy to Use Pads

  1. Place the pad in a designated area – a quiet, accessible spot (not near food or water).
  2. Take your puppy to the pad frequently – after meals, playtime, and waking up.
  3. Reward success – praise and treat when your puppy eliminates on the pad.
  4. Gradually reduce the pad area – once your puppy is consistent, reduce the number of pads to encourage outdoor elimination.
  5. Transition to outdoor potty – after a few weeks, move the pad closer to the door and eventually outside.

5. Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Placing the pad too far from your puppy's resting area – they won't have time to reach it.
  • Not replacing pads frequently – soiled pads should be changed to prevent odor.
  • Using scented pads – strong fragrances may discourage elimination.
  • Expecting perfection too soon – accidents are part of the process.
