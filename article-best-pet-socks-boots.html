1. Why Paw Protection Matters

  • Hot pavement burns – summer pavement can burn paw pads in minutes.
  • Winter salt and ice – salt and de‑icing chemicals irritate paws; ice can cut pads.
  • Rough terrain – hiking on rocks, gravel, or rough surfaces.
  • Post‑surgery or injury – protecting surgical sites or wounds.
  • Senior pets with mobility issues – extra traction prevents slipping.

4. How to Measure for the Right Fit

  • Place your dog's paw on paper and trace it. Measure the width and length.
  • Add 1‑2 cm for movement. Measure the height of the paw up to the ankle joint.
  • Check size charts for each brand – sizes vary significantly.

5. How to Train Your Dog to Wear Boots

  1. Introduce one boot at a time with treats and praise.
  2. Keep the boot on for a few seconds, then remove and reward.
  3. Gradually increase time with all four boots on.
  4. Start walking indoors on carpet (more traction) before moving outdoors.
  5. Be patient – some dogs take a few weeks to adjust.

6. What to Avoid

  • Boots that are too tight – can restrict circulation or cause chafing.
  • Boots that are too loose – will slip off or cause tripping.
  • Socks without non‑skid bottoms – can cause slipping on hard floors.
  • Low‑quality materials – cheap boots may not be waterproof or durable.
