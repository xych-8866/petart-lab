2. How Flea Collars Work

Flea collars release active ingredients (such as flumethrin or deltamethrin) that repel and kill fleas and ticks. Some collars also contain insect growth regulators to prevent eggs from hatching. The collar works through contact – fleas and ticks don't need to bite to die, they just need to touch the collar or the pet's fur.

3. How Insect Sprays Work

Insect repellent sprays contain active ingredients that repel fleas, ticks, and other insects. Some sprays are designed for direct application to your pet (pets safe), while others are for the environment (home and yard). Apply before outdoor activities like hiking, camping, or walks in wooded areas.

4. Safety Considerations

  • For cats: Some flea collars are toxic to cats. Always choose products specifically labeled for cats.
  • For dogs: Some sprays contain ingredients that are safe for dogs but not for cats.
  • All pets: Monitor for signs of irritation or allergic reactions.
  • Children: Keep products out of reach of children.
⚠️ Permethrin-based products are highly toxic to cats. Never use a dog flea collar or dog spray on a cat.

6. How to Choose the Right Option

  • For year-round prevention: Choose a flea collar (e.g., Seresto) for continuous protection.
  • For outdoor activities: Use an insect spray before walks, hikes, or camping.
  • For multi-pet households: Ensure products are safe for all pets (separate dog and cat products).
  • For budget-conscious owners: Consider affordable flea collars or sprays.

8. What to Avoid

  • Using dog flea products on cats – can be toxic
  • Cheap, low-quality collars – may not be effective and can cause skin irritation
  • Over-applying sprays – can cause irritation
  • Ignoring signs of fleas – can lead to tapeworms and skin infections

9. Your Action Plan
