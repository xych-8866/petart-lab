2. What to Look For

  • Front‑clip attachment – essential for discouraging pulling.
  • Dual attachment points – offers training and casual walking options.
  • Adjustable straps – ensures a secure, comfortable fit.
  • Padding – prevents chafing, especially for short‑haired breeds.
  • Durable materials – metal hardware and strong nylon webbing.
  • Reflective stitching – for low‑light visibility.

4. How to Fit a Harness Correctly

  • Front (chest) strap – should sit low on the chest, not pressing against the throat.
  • Belly strap – should be snug but not tight (you should be able to slide two fingers under it).
  • Check for rubbing – especially under the armpits, which can cause chafing.
  • Adjust as needed – after a few wears, check the fit; harnesses can loosen over time.

5. How to Train Your Dog to Walk Calmly with a Harness

  1. Introduce the harness at home with treats and positive reinforcement.
  2. Start indoors with short, calm walks on a loose leash.
  3. Use the front clip – when your dog pulls, the harness gently turns them sideways, and you call them back to your side.
  4. Reward calm walking with treats and praise.
  5. Gradually increase distractions (outdoors, other dogs, traffic).

6. What to Avoid

  • Harnesses that restrict movement – some designs can cause gait issues.
  • Cheap materials – can break or cause chafing.
  • Improper fit – can cause discomfort or injury.
  • Using a harness as a restraint – harnesses should be used for training, not punishment.
