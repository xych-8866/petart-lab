Every day, millions of plastic bags are used to pick up dog waste – most of them end up in landfills, taking centuries to degrade. Switching to biodegradable or compostable bags is a simple way to reduce your environmental footprint while still being a responsible pet owner.

🌍 Fact: An estimated 10‑20 billion dog waste bags are used annually in the US alone. Most are made from virgin plastic and never degrade.

1. What to Look For

FeatureWhy It Matters
Leak‑proofDouble‑sealed seams prevent leaks and keep your hands clean.
Biodegradable / CompostableMade from plant‑based materials (cornstarch, PLA) that break down faster.
ThicknessAt least 15‑20 microns for strength; thin bags tear easily.
SizeFor large dogs, choose XL bags (9‑12 inches wide). For small dogs, standard size works.
Easy‑open tabsMakes dispensing from a roll quick and easy (especially with gloves on).
FragranceChoose unscented if your pet is sensitive to smells.

3. How to Choose the Right Size

  • Small dogs (under 20 lbs): Standard size (7‑8 inches wide) works well.
  • Medium dogs (20‑50 lbs): Medium or large (8‑10 inches wide).
  • Large dogs (50+ lbs): XL (10‑12 inches wide) to prevent overflow.

4. Accessories to Consider

  • Dispenser clips – attach to your leash for easy access.
  • Poop bag holder (pouch) – holds a roll and often includes a pocket for treats.
  • Small trash can with lid – for outdoor use to collect waste before disposal.
  • Odor‑control disposal systems – sealed containers that trap smells (e.g., Diaper Genie but for dog waste).

5. Eco‑Friendly Disposal Tips

  • Check local regulations – some areas allow compostable bags in green bins; others don't.
  • Never flush dog waste – it can cause water contamination.
  • If you have a garden – consider a dog waste composter (digester) that breaks waste down without composting (e.g., Doggie Dooley).

6. What to Avoid

  • Cheap, thin bags – they tear easily, leading to messy hands.
  • Non‑biodegradable plastic – most standard bags are made from virgin plastic and take centuries to decompose.
  • Heavily scented bags – some pets are sensitive to artificial fragrances.
