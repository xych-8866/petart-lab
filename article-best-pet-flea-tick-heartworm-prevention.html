1. Why Prevention Matters (Even Indoors)

  • Fleas: Cause itching, tapeworms, anemia, and flea allergy dermatitis. They hitchhike on shoes and clothing.
  • Ticks: Transmit Lyme disease, Ehrlichia, Anaplasma, and Rocky Mountain spotted fever.
  • Heartworms: Transmitted by mosquitoes. Can cause coughing, exercise intolerance, and heart failure. Treatment is expensive ($1,000‑$2,000) and risky.
⚠️ Myth: "My pet never goes outside." Fleas can still enter via other pets, humans, or open windows. Year‑round prevention is the safest approach.

2. Types of Prevention (Oral, Topical, Collar)

  • Oral chews (e.g., NexGard, Bravecto, Simparica): Easy to give, no greasy residue, kills fleas/ticks within hours. Requires prescription. Best for dogs who swim or bathe frequently.
  • Topical liquids (e.g., Frontline Plus, Advantage): Applied to skin monthly. Effective but can cause temporary greasiness. Some pets hate the sensation.
  • Seresto collar: 8‑month protection, odorless, water‑resistant. Great for cats and dogs. Must be fitted properly.
  • Heartworm‑only products (e.g., Heartgard, Interceptor): Monthly chewable that prevents heartworms. Often combined with flea/tick in all‑in‑one products (e.g., Simparica Trio, Revolution Plus).

6. What to Avoid

  • Cheap, unregulated products from unknown brands – may contain harmful ingredients or be ineffective.
  • Using dog products on cats – permethrin (in many dog topicals) is toxic to cats.
  • Skipping winter months – fleas and ticks can survive indoors and on wildlife year‑round.
