📊 Fact: Overgrown nails can cause joint pain, arthritis, and even lead to changes in gait. Regular nail trimming (every 2‑4 weeks) is essential for your pet's mobility and comfort.

1. Why Nail Care Matters

  • Prevents joint pain – overgrown nails force the paw into an unnatural position.
  • Prevents splitting and breaking – long nails can crack or split, causing pain.
  • Reduces risk of ingrown nails – nails can curl into the paw pad.
  • Protects your floors – trimmed nails are less likely to scratch.
  • Improves comfort – walking becomes easier and more comfortable.

4. How to Trim Your Pet's Nails Safely

  1. Desensitize your pet – handle paws gently and reward with treats.
  2. Choose the right tool – clippers for a quick cut, grinder for smoothing.
  3. Cut at a 45‑degree angle – avoid cutting the quick (pink part in light nails; dark oval in dark nails).
  4. Use the grinder for finishing – smooth rough edges and round the tip.
  5. Apply styptic powder – if bleeding occurs, apply pressure with styptic powder to stop it.

6. What to Avoid

  • Using dull clippers – can crush the nail and cause pain.
  • Cutting too much at once – take small increments to avoid the quick.
  • Forgetting styptic powder – always have it ready.
  • Rushing – take your time, especially with anxious pets.
