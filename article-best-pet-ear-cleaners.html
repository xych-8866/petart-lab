1. Why Routine Ear Cleaning Matters

  • Prevents infections – removes wax, debris, and moisture that harbor bacteria and yeast.
  • Reduces odor – foul smells indicate infection.
  • Improves comfort – prevents itching and scratching.
  • Detects problems early – you'll notice changes in the ear canal before they become severe.

4. How to Clean Your Pet's Ears (Step‑by‑Step)

  1. Hold your pet's ear flap up and gently fill the ear canal with a vet‑approved ear cleaner.
  2. Massage the base of the ear gently for 30‑60 seconds (you'll hear a squishing sound).
  3. Allow your pet to shake their head – this helps bring debris to the outer ear.
  4. Wipe the outer ear with a cotton ball or gauze (never use cotton swabs inside the ear).
  5. Repeat on the other ear with a fresh cotton ball.

Frequency: For floppy‑eared dogs or pets prone to infections, clean weekly. For others, every 2‑4 weeks.

5. What to Avoid

  • Cotton swabs inside the ear – pushes debris deeper and can damage the eardrum.
  • Alcohol or vinegar – can irritate inflamed tissue.
  • Ignoring signs of infection – redness, odor, pain, or discharge.
  • Using human ear drops – may contain ingredients harmful to pets.
