1. Why Toy Storage Matters

  • Safety – prevents tripping hazards and accidental ingestion
  • Organization – keeps toys accessible and easy to find
  • Durability – protects toys from damage
  • Aesthetics – keeps your home tidy
  • Teaching – encourages your pet to put toys away (with training)

4. How to Choose the Right Storage

  • Consider space – choose storage that fits your home layout
  • Consider durability – choose materials that can withstand your pet
  • Consider accessibility – your pet should be able to access the storage (with training)
  • Consider style – choose design that matches your home decor

5. How to Train Your Pet to Use a Toy Box

  1. Place a toy in the box and reward your pet with treats when they put toys in
  2. Use commands like "put it away" or "toy box"
  3. Start with a small number of toys and gradually increase
  4. Be consistent and patient – it may take a few weeks

7. What to Avoid

  • Sharp corners – can injure your pet
  • Toxic materials – choose BPA-free, non-toxic materials
  • Too-small compartments – large toys may not fit
  • Non-breathable materials – can cause odors
