2. What to Look For

  • Durable materials – stainless steel, aluminum, or brass for long‑lasting tags.
  • Clear engraving – deep, legible lettering that won't wear off.
  • Secure fastening – split rings, S‑hooks, or safety breakaway clips.
  • Size appropriate – tags should not be too heavy or large for your pet.
  • Visibility – brightly colored or reflective tags help with visibility.
  • Updateable information – choose QR code or smart tags for easy updates.

4. How to Fit a Collar Correctly

  • Two‑finger rule – you should be able to slide two fingers between the collar and your pet's neck.
  • Not too tight – should not cause hair loss or rubbing.
  • Not too loose – should not slip over the head (except breakaway collars).
  • Check regularly – collars can loosen over time; adjust as needed.

6. What to Avoid

  • Tags that are too heavy – can cause neck strain, especially for small dogs and cats.
  • Poorly engraved tags – can become illegible within months.
  • Non‑breakaway collars for cats – can be dangerous if the cat gets caught.
  • Tags with outdated information – always keep your contact information current.
