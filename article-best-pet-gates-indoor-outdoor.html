1. Pressure‑Mounted vs. Hardware‑Mounted Gates

  • Pressure‑mounted: Fits by pressing against walls/doorframes. Easy to install, no drilling. **Not safe for top of stairs** (can be knocked over). Best for room‑to‑room or bottom of stairs.
  • Hardware‑mounted: Screwed into wall studs or banisters. Permanent or semi‑permanent. **Required for top of stairs**. Strongest option for large dogs.

5. What to Avoid

  • Gates with horizontal bars – pets (and children) can climb them like ladders.
  • Pressure‑mounted gates at top of stairs – they can dislodge and cause a fall.
  • Gates that are too short – most medium/large dogs can jump 30‑inch gates. Choose at least 36 inches.
  • Cheap plastic gates – easily broken by determined dogs.

6. Gate Height & Dog Size Guide

  • Small dogs (<25 lbs): 24‑30 inches
  • Medium dogs (25‑50 lbs): 30‑36 inches
  • Large dogs (50‑90 lbs): 36‑38 inches
  • Extra large (>90 lbs) or jumpers: 38‑42 inches or use a playpen with vertical bars
