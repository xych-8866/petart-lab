1. What Is a Lick Mat?

A lick mat is a textured mat made of food-grade silicone or rubber. It has patterns (ridges, dots, or spirals) that trap food, requiring your pet to lick to get it out. This action stimulates the "licking reflex" and provides a calming effect.

2. Benefits of Lick Mats

  • Reduces anxiety: Licking releases endorphins that calm the nervous system
  • Slows eating: Helps pets eat more slowly, reducing bloat and improving digestion
  • Mental enrichment: Provides a puzzle-solving activity
  • Bonding: Great for training and creating positive associations
  • Dental benefits: Licking can help clean the tongue and promote dental health

5. What to Put on a Lick Mat

  • Peanut butter – sugar-free, xylitol-free (check ingredients!)
  • Canned pumpkin – plain, unsweetened
  • Plain yogurt – unsweetened, with live cultures
  • Wet food or kibble paste – mixed with water
  • Mashed banana or sweet potato
  • Kitten formula or broth – for cats

6. How to Use a Lick Mat

  1. Spread a thin layer of the chosen food evenly over the mat
  2. Secure the mat on a flat surface (use suction cups if available)
  3. Supervise your pet while they lick
  4. Wash the mat after each use (dishwasher or hot soapy water)

8. What to Avoid

  • Xylitol or artificial sweeteners – toxic to dogs
  • Garlic or onions – toxic to dogs and cats
  • Leaving the mat out for too long – food can spoil
  • Overfeeding – use lick mats as a treat, not a full meal
