☔ Did you know? Dogs with short coats (Boxers, Greyhounds, Dobermans) lose body heat 3‑4x faster than double‑coated breeds in wet conditions.

1. Why Your Dog Needs a Raincoat (Not Just an Umbrella)

  • Prevents hypothermia – wet fur pulls heat away from the body.
  • Reduces skin issues – trapped moisture can lead to hot spots and fungal infections.
  • Keeps them willing to walk – a dry dog is a happy walker.
  • Easy cleanup – wipe the coat, not your dog.

2. Features to Look For

  • Waterproof (not just water‑resistant) fabric – PVC or heavy nylon with sealed seams.
  • Belly coverage – for low‑to‑ground dogs (Corgis, Dachshunds).
  • Reflective strips – critical for low‑light rainy days.
  • Leash hole – positioned on the back for harness attachment.
  • Adjustable straps – Velcro or snap closures for a snug but not tight fit.
  • Breathable lining – prevents overheating.

4. How to Measure Your Dog for a Raincoat

  • Back length: From base of neck (where collar sits) to base of tail. This is the most critical measurement.
  • Chest girth: Around the widest part of the ribcage, behind front legs.
  • Neck girth: Around where a collar would sit.

Check the brand's size chart – sizes vary significantly between manufacturers.

6. Care & Maintenance

  • Rinse with cold water after muddy walks – don't use a washing machine (damages waterproof coating).
  • Hang dry indoors – never machine dry.
  • Reapply waterproof spray after 10‑15 washes – Nikwax Tech Wash or similar.
