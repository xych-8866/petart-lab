1. Why Fast Eating Is Dangerous

  • Bloat (GDV) risk – rapid eating increases air intake, which can cause stomach torsion.
  • Vomiting and regurgitation – undigested food comes back up.
  • Obesity – fast eaters often don't register fullness, leading to overeating.
  • Choking hazard – large kibble can get stuck in the esophagus.

2. How Slow Feeders Work

Slow feeders have obstacles (maze patterns, ridges, spirals) that force your dog to work around them to get to the food, extending meal time from seconds to minutes.

4. How to Transition Your Dog to a Slow Feeder

  1. Start with the easiest level (mild maze pattern).
  2. Place the new bowl next to their old bowl for a few days.
  3. Try feeding one meal in the slow feeder, one in the old bowl.
  4. If your dog gets frustrated, add wet food or broth to make the food more accessible initially.
  5. Gradually increase difficulty as they get used to it.

5. What to Avoid

  • BPA‑free plastic – always check (most are BPA‑free).
  • Hard‑to‑clean designs – choose dishwasher‑safe or easy‑to‑scrub patterns.
  • Small bowl for large breeds – may not hold enough food.
  • No non‑slip base – can slide around, frustrating your dog.
