📊 Fact: Elevated feeders can help dogs with arthritis, neck pain, or mobility issues. However, they are not recommended for dogs at risk of bloat (GDV) unless specifically recommended by a vet.

1. When to Consider Elevated Feeding

  • Senior dogs with arthritis – reduces neck and back strain.
  • Large breeds with neck pain – less bending.
  • Dogs with joint or spinal issues – eases posture.
  • Dogs with digestive issues (megaesophagus) – allows gravity to aid swallowing.
  • Not recommended for bloat‑prone breeds – some studies suggest elevated bowls may increase bloat risk.

4. How to Choose the Right Height

  • Measure from the floor to your dog's withers (shoulder height).
  • General rule: The bowl should be at the level of the dog's lower chest (not neck).
  • For most dogs, the bowl height should be 10‑15% of their shoulder height.

6. What to Avoid

  • Stands that are too high – can cause neck strain.
  • Non‑adjustable stands – may not fit your pet as they grow or age.
  • Plastic bowls – can harbor bacteria; stainless steel or ceramic is better.
  • Unstable stands – can tip over and cause spills.
