1. How Clicker Training Works

  • Step 1: Condition the clicker – Click, then treat. Repeat 10‑20 times. Your dog learns that the click means a reward is coming.
  • Step 2: Mark the desired behavior – Click at the exact moment your dog performs the behavior you want.
  • Step 3: Reward – Immediately follow the click with a treat.
  • Step 4: Repeat and generalize – Practice in different environments with increasing distractions.

The clicker marks the behavior, not the dog. It provides precise feedback and eliminates the confusion of timing.

4. How to Start Clicker Training (Step‑by‑Step)

  1. Charge the clicker – Click, treat, click, treat, 10‑20 times. Your dog will learn that the click predicts a reward.
  2. Shape the behavior – Break down the desired behavior into small steps. For "sit," start by clicking when your dog's rear lowers even slightly.
  3. Be precise – Click at the exact moment of the behavior. A fraction of a second makes a difference.
  4. Keep sessions short – 5‑10 minutes, 3‑4 times per day.
  5. End on a positive note – Stop when your dog is successful, not when they're frustrated.

6. What to Avoid

  • Clicking and not rewarding – the click must always be followed by a treat.
  • Clicking too late – the click should mark the exact moment of the behavior, not after.
  • Using the clicker as a "come here" or "leave it" cue – the clicker is a marker, not a command.
  • Training when you're frustrated – dogs sense your mood; keep sessions positive.
