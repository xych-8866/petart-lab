1. Why Interactive Toys Work

Dogs need to use their brains. Puzzle toys and treat‑dispensing toys simulate problem‑solving, which tires them out more effectively than a long walk. Benefits include:

  • Reduces separation anxiety and boredom
  • Slows down fast eaters (puzzle feeders)
  • Strengthens the bond between you and your dog
  • Provides low‑impact exercise for senior dogs

3. How to Introduce Puzzle Toys to Your Dog

  • Start easy: Leave a few treats in open compartments so your dog gets immediate success.
  • Increase difficulty gradually: Once they master level 1, add blocks or sliding covers.
  • Supervise initially: Some dogs may try to chew the plastic (replace with heavy‑duty options if needed).
  • Rotate toys: Keep a set of 3‑4 toys and swap them every few days to maintain novelty.

5. What to Avoid

  • Cheap puzzle toys with small pieces: They can break off and become a choking hazard.
  • Toys that are too difficult: If your dog gets frustrated, they'll lose interest. Start at level 1.
  • Non‑dishwasher safe: These toys get slimy and must be cleaned regularly.
