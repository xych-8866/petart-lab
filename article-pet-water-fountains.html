1. Why Pets Prefer Running Water

In nature, still water can harbor bacteria. Cats, especially, have a low thirst drive and instinctively seek moving water. A fountain mimics a fresh stream, encouraging your pet to drink more. Benefits include:

  • Supports kidney function and prevents chronic kidney disease
  • Reduces urinary crystal formation (common in cats on dry food)
  • Helps prevent constipation and dehydration
  • Lessens bad breath (bacteria flushed out)

3. How to Train Your Picky Pet to Use a Fountain

  1. Place the fountain next to their old water bowl (turned off).
  2. After 2‑3 days, fill it with water and turn it on while they're eating.
  3. If they're scared of the sound, start with the lowest flow setting or remove the spout so only the pool shows.
  4. Reward with treats when they approach or sniff the fountain.
  5. Once they drink, remove the old bowl entirely.

4. Maintenance (Critical for Longevity & Safety)

  • Change water every 2‑3 days – even in a fountain.
  • Replace filters every 2‑4 weeks (follow manufacturer).
  • Clean pump weekly – disassemble and rinse with warm water to prevent slime buildup.
  • Deep clean with mild soap (no harsh chemicals) every 2 weeks.
  • Replace the entire fountain every 12‑18 months (plastic can scratch, harboring bacteria).

6. What to Avoid

  • All‑plastic fountains – scratch easily, harbor bacteria, can cause feline acne.
  • Hard‑to‑clean corners – if you can't reach every surface, biofilm will form.
  • Proprietary filters that are expensive to replace – check filter cost before buying.
  • Cheap pumps that are loud – look for "ultra‑quiet" in reviews.
