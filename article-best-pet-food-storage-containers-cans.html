1. Why Proper Food Storage Matters

  • Preserves nutrients – moisture, heat, and air degrade vitamins and fats.
  • Prevents spoilage – seals out moisture and oxygen, which cause food to go stale.
  • Keeps pests out – ants, weevils, and rodents can infiltrate open bags.
  • Reduces odor – sealed containers contain food smells.
  • Convenience – easy to scoop and pour.

4. How to Choose the Right Size

  • Small pets (under 15 lbs) – 5‑10 lb capacity containers.
  • Medium pets (15‑50 lbs) – 15‑30 lb capacity containers.
  • Large pets (50+ lbs) – 40‑60 lb capacity containers.
  • Consider buying a container that holds the entire bag of food to minimize transfer of air.

5. How to Store Pet Food Correctly

  1. Leave food in its original bag – the bag has important information (batch number, expiration date). Place the bag inside the container.
  2. Seal tightly – after each use, close the lid completely to preserve freshness.
  3. Store in a cool, dry place – avoid direct sunlight and extreme heat.
  4. Clean the container regularly – wash with warm, soapy water between bags to prevent buildup.

7. What to Avoid

  • Non‑airtight containers – allow moisture and pests to enter.
  • Plastic containers that are not BPA‑free – can leach chemicals into food.
  • Containers that are hard to clean – can harbor bacteria.
  • Leaving food in the original bag without an outer container – can lead to staleness and pest problems.

8. Your Action Plan

  1. Choose a container that fits your typical food bag size.
  2. Ensure it has an airtight seal and is BPA‑free.
  3. Transfer food (with the bag) into the container immediately after opening.
  4. Clean the container between each bag of food.
