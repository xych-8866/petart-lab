1. What Makes a Great Pet Camera?

  • 1080p or higher resolution – to clearly see your pet’s face and actions.
  • Two‑way audio – talk to and hear your pet.
  • Treat dispenser – remotely launch a treat to reward calm behavior.
  • Night vision – monitor in low light (most pets are active at dawn/dusk).
  • Motion & sound alerts – get notified when your pet is active or barking/howling.
  • Cloud or SD card recording – capture moments to review later.
  • Pet detection AI – some cameras can distinguish pets from humans or other motion.

3. How to Use a Pet Camera to Reduce Separation Anxiety

  • Practice “alone time” while you’re still home. Leave the room, watch your pet’s reaction, and reward calm behavior via treat toss.
  • Talk in a soothing voice. Use the same phrase each time (“I’ll be right back, stay calm”).
  • Don’t react to whining or barking immediately. Wait for a moment of quiet, then reward. This teaches that calmness brings treats.
  • Combine with puzzle toys. A treat‑dispensing camera + a Kong or snuffle mat keeps your pet occupied.

5. Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Using treats too often. If your pet gets treats for barking, they’ll bark more. Reward quiet moments only.
  • Placing the camera too high. You need to see your pet’s face and body language. Place at pet level or slightly above.
  • Ignoring app permissions. Grant the camera app notifications, or you’ll miss alerts.
  • Not securing the camera. Some dogs may knock it over if it’s lightweight. Use a wall mount or heavy base.

6. Subscription vs. Local Storage – Which Is Right for You?
