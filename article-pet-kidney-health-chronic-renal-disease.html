📊 Prevalence: Chronic kidney disease (CKD) affects approximately 30‑50% of cats over 15 years and 10‑20% of senior dogs. It's one of the leading causes of death in older pets.

1. Early Signs (Don't Ignore These)

  • Increased thirst and urination – drinking more, needing to go out more often, accidents in the house.
  • Weight loss – despite normal or increased appetite.
  • Bad breath (uremic breath) – ammonia‑like odor from waste buildup.
  • Vomiting, lethargy, poor coat – later stage signs.
⚠️ Early detection saves lives. Ask your vet for a senior wellness blood panel (BUN, creatinine, SDMA) annually for pets over 7 years.

2. Prevention: Hydration & Diet

The single most important preventive measure is adequate water intake. Cats, especially, have a low thirst drive. Encourage drinking with:

  • Pet water fountains – moving water attracts pets.
  • Wet food – contains 75‑85% moisture vs. 10% in dry kibble.
  • Multiple water stations – place bowls in different rooms.

4. Key Supplements for Kidney Support

  • Omega‑3 fatty acids (EPA/DHA) – reduce inflammation and slow fibrosis.
  • Probiotics (Azodyl) – help lower blood urea nitrogen (BUN) by metabolizing waste in the gut.
  • B vitamins – lost through excess urination; supplementation helps energy and appetite.
  • Phosphate binders – given with meals to reduce phosphorus absorption.

7. What to Avoid

  • High‑protein diets (unless early stage and vet‑approved) – excess protein increases kidney workload.
  • NSAIDs (ibuprofen, aspirin) – can cause acute kidney failure.
  • Dehydration – even mild dehydration worsens kidney values.

8. Your 3‑Step Action Plan

  1. Schedule senior bloodwork – for pets over 7, include BUN, creatinine, SDMA, and urinalysis.
  2. Switch to a kidney‑friendly diet – prescription renal diet or low‑phosphorus over‑the‑counter option.
  3. Increase water intake – add a fountain, feed wet food, and encourage drinking.

Final thought: Chronic kidney disease is not a death sentence. With early detection, dietary management, and supportive care, many pets live years of quality life. The cost of a senior blood panel ($150‑$250) is a small price for peace of mind and early intervention.
