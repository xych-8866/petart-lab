2. What to Look For

  • Size – your pet should be able to stretch out fully.
  • Material – durable, easy to clean, and non‑toxic.
  • Washability – removable, machine‑washable covers make maintenance easy.
  • Support – memory foam or orthopedic support for senior pets.
  • Non‑slip base – keeps the bed stable on hard floors.

4. How to Choose the Right Bed for Your Pet

  • Senior pets – orthopedic memory foam to reduce joint pressure.
  • Anxious pets – bolstered or cave‑style beds for a sense of security.
  • Warm climates – elevated or cooling beds.
  • Cold climates – self‑warming or fleece‑lined beds.
  • For crates – fitted mats or flat beds that fit the crate floor.

6. What to Avoid

  • Cheap foam – flattens quickly and provides little support.
  • Non‑washable covers – can harbor bacteria and odors.
  • Beds that are too small – your pet can't stretch out, leading to discomfort.
  • Beds with loose stitching – can be chewed and ingested.

7. Your Action Plan

  1. Assess your pet's sleeping habits and any health issues (arthritis, anxiety).
  2. Choose a bed that matches their needs (orthopedic, bolstered, cooling, or heated).
  3. Ensure the bed is easy to clean (removable, washable cover).
  4. Place the bed in a quiet, comfortable location.
