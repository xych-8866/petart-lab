How to Choose the Best Dog Food for Your Senior Dog

By PetArt Lab · May 23, 2026

Key Takeaways

  • Switch to senior-specific formulas around age 7
  • Look for glucosamine for joint support
  • Avoid fillers like corn, soy, and artificial additives
  • Consider wet food if your dog has dental issues

Your senior dog's nutritional needs change with age. The food that kept them healthy as an active adult may no longer be ideal. Here's how to pick the right senior dog food.

1. Senior-Specific Formulas Exist for a Reason

Around age 7 (depending on breed), most dogs benefit from switching to a senior formula. These foods typically have fewer calories to match a slower metabolism, higher fiber for digestive health, and added joint-supporting nutrients.

2. What to Avoid

Steer clear of foods where corn, soy, or wheat appear high on the ingredient list. These are fillers with little nutritional value. Also avoid artificial colors, flavors, and preservatives.

3. Dry vs. Wet Food

If your senior dog has dental problems, wet food can be easier to eat. You can also soften dry kibble with warm water. Some owners mix both for the best of both worlds.
