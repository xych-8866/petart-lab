How to Choose the Best Pet Insurance for Your Pet

By PetArt Lab · May 30, 2026

Key Takeaways

  • Understand the three main types: accident-only, accident & illness, and wellness plans
  • Look for policies that cover hereditary conditions and chronic illnesses
  • Compare deductibles, reimbursement rates, and annual limits
  • Check if the insurance has a waiting period and any exclusions

Pet insurance can save you thousands in unexpected veterinary bills, but not all policies are created equal. With so many providers and plans, it's hard to know what's best for your pet. This guide breaks down what you need to look for.

1. Types of Pet Insurance Plans

Most providers offer three levels of coverage:

  • Accident-only: Covers injuries like broken bones, cuts, or swallowing objects. Cheapest but limited.
  • Accident & Illness: The most common plan. Covers accidents plus infections, cancer, digestive issues, etc.
  • Wellness/Preventive: Optional add-on for routine care like vaccinations, dental cleanings, and flea prevention.

2. Key Terms to Understand

Deductible: The amount you pay before insurance kicks in. Higher deductibles = lower monthly premiums. Reimbursement rate: Typically 70%, 80%, or 90% of covered costs. Annual limit: Maximum payout per year ($5k, $10k, unlimited). Choose based on your risk tolerance.

3. What to Watch Out For

Avoid policies that exclude hereditary conditions (common in purebreds), have long waiting periods (>30 days for illnesses), or require you to use specific vets. Most good insurances let you visit any licensed vet.

4. When to Buy Pet Insurance

Insure your pet when they're young and healthy – pre-existing conditions are almost never covered. Even puppies and kittens can get insured. For older pets, compare policies that have shorter waiting periods or no upper age limits.
