Key Takeaways

  • Specialized hairball control foods contain fiber blends that help pass fur naturally

If you've ever stepped on a slimy hairball in the middle of the night, you know the struggle is real. While occasional hairballs are normal for cats, frequent hacking, gagging, or vomiting could mean your cat needs extra digestive support. Here are the most effective solutions that actually work.

1. Hairball Control Cat Food

The right diet makes a huge difference. Look for formulas with specific fiber blends—beet pulp, cellulose, and psyllium husk help move hair through the digestive tract naturally. Some foods also contain omega-3 and omega-6 fatty acids for skin and coat health, which can reduce shedding at the source.

3. Regular Brushing: The Best Prevention

The most effective way to reduce hairballs is to remove loose fur before your cat swallows it. Brush long-haired cats daily, short-haired cats 2-3 times per week. During spring and fall shedding seasons, increase frequency. A good slicker brush or deshedding tool makes a noticeable difference.

4. When to See the Vet

If your cat is gagging frequently without producing a hairball, vomiting more than once a month, losing appetite, or showing signs of constipation, schedule a vet visit. These could indicate a blockage or underlying health issue that needs professional attention.
