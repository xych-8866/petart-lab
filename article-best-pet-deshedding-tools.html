1. How Deshedding Tools Work

Deshedding tools have specially designed blades or teeth that reach deep into the undercoat, catching and removing loose hair without cutting the topcoat. This reduces shedding and prevents mats.

  • Furminator-style tools – use a metal edge with fine teeth to pull out loose undercoat.
  • Undercoat rakes – have widely spaced teeth to penetrate thick undercoats.
  • Deshedding gloves – have rubber nubs that collect loose hair while massaging.

4. How to Use a Deshedding Tool

  1. Brush your pet with a standard brush to remove surface tangles.
  2. Use the deshedding tool in the direction of hair growth, with light pressure.
  3. Work in sections, brushing each area 3-5 times.
  4. Clean the tool between strokes to remove collected hair.
  5. Be gentle – avoid overbrushing, which can irritate the skin.

5. How Often to Deshed

  • Shedding season (spring/fall): 2-3 times per week.
  • Maintenance: Once a week for most breeds.
  • Double-coated breeds: Daily during shedding season.

7. What to Avoid

  • Using too much pressure – can irritate the skin and cause brush burn.
  • Deshedding dry, flaky skin – can cause discomfort.
  • Overusing on sensitive areas – avoid the tail, ears, and bony areas.
  • Using a tool that cuts the topcoat – can damage the coat permanently.
