How to Brush Your Dog's Teeth: A Step-by-Step Guide

By PetArt Lab · May 29, 2026

Key Takeaways

  • Use dog-specific toothpaste—never human toothpaste
  • Start slow and make it a positive experience
  • Aim for daily brushing, but even 2-3 times per week helps
  • Dental chews and toys are supplements, not replacements

Did you know that over 80% of dogs show signs of dental disease by age three? Poor oral hygiene doesn't just cause bad breath—it can lead to serious health problems like heart and kidney disease. The good news: brushing your dog's teeth is easier than you think when you follow the right steps.

1. Gather the Right Supplies

You need two essentials: a dog-specific toothbrush and dog toothpaste. Never use human toothpaste—it contains xylitol and fluoride, which are toxic to dogs. Dog toothpaste comes in flavors like poultry and peanut butter, making the experience much more enjoyable for your pup.

2. Start Slow and Build Trust

Week 1: Let your dog lick toothpaste off your finger. Week 2: Gently rub your finger along their gums. Week 3: Introduce the toothbrush with toothpaste on it, letting them lick and chew. Week 4: Begin gentle brushing in circular motions along the gumline. Always reward with praise and treats after each session.

3. The Brushing Technique

Focus on the outer surfaces of the teeth where plaque builds up most. Hold the brush at a 45-degree angle to the gumline. Use gentle circular motions. Aim for 30 seconds per side. Don't worry about the inside surfaces—your dog's tongue naturally cleans those.

4. How Often Should You Brush?

Daily brushing is ideal, but even 2-3 times per week significantly reduces plaque and tartar. Pair brushing with dental chews, water additives, and annual professional cleanings for the best results.
