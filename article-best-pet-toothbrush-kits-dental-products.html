3. How to Brush Your Pet's Teeth (Step‑by‑Step)

  1. Introduce the toothpaste as a treat – let your pet lick it off your finger.
  2. Gradually introduce the brush (or finger brush) with toothpaste.
  3. Start with the outside surfaces of the teeth (the tongue cleans the inside).
  4. Use gentle circular motions, focusing on the gumline.
  5. Reward with praise and a treat after each session.

5. What to Avoid

  • Human toothpaste – contains xylitol (toxic) and fluoride (upset stomach).
  • Hard bristles – can damage gums and enamel.
  • Baking soda or DIY pastes – can be abrasive and cause enamel erosion.
  • Forcing brushing – can create lifelong resistance.

Final thought: A toothbrush kit costs $10‑$20 and lasts for months. Daily brushing is the most effective way to prevent costly dental procedures and keep your pet healthy. Start slowly and reward your pet – you'll both benefit from a healthier mouth.
